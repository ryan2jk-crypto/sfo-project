Flying over SFO

Domestic and International Flights (2008-2018)

San Francisco International Airport has been served as a gateway to the United States for a long time. Due to its geological proximity to Asia and South America, the airport is a hub for many airlines including United Airlines, American Airlines, and other international airlines. Thus, I suggest the theme ‘mobility’ fits really well to this dataset and future visualizations. San Francisco is a city concentrating many of the incoming passengers from Asia and South America and redistributing them into smaller cities in the states. This visualization shows how air traffic in and out of San Francisco changed over the last decade. With the series of visualizations you can check the relationship between domestic/international traffic, development of low-fare airlines, trend of mobility between continents, and efficiency of domestic and international routes (by comparing flight frequency and the actual amount of users).
